Slopes of Lines and Curves

The Slope of a Line​

Linear functions produce straight-line graphs. In general, a straight line follows the following equation:

y=a+bxy = a + bxy=a+bx

where aaa and bbb are fixed numbers.

The line on the graph is the set of points:

[(x,y):x,y∈R,y=a+bx]\left[ (x,y): x,y \in \mathbb{R}, y = a+bx \right][(x,y):x,y∈R,y=a+bx]

Details​

The slope of a straight line represents the change in the yyy coordinate corresponding to a unit change in the xxx coordinate.
